Some information on the project

Renaturing the Aa River

In recent decades greater awareness for the sensitivity and the importance of river ecosystems has emerged in trans-national governmental institutions from national to local level. This lead to the initiation of renaturation procedures for rivers under the European Union water policies: EU Water Framework Directive (WFD) with “good quantitative status” and “good chemical status” of all rivers until 2023.

Major challenges that rivers and lakes in Europe face are grouped into: water availability and supply challenges, water quality issues, structural changes to and physical modifications of rivers and lakes (EU, 2016). In 2008 the municipal government of Münster authorised the renaturation of the Aa river, which flows through the city. These renaturation measures will help restore a section of the Aa to its natural state.

Monitoring and exploratory approaches to assess the effects on vegetation and hydromorphological quality such as river bank structure, river continuity and/ or substrate of the river bed, turned out to be a suitable and assertive opportunity to gain knowledge by using geospatial technologies in a practical context.

The teams

Image analysis and 3D

Provision of meaningful spatial products and services is integral in the process of monitoring the conservation and renaturation of the environment. This project group aimed to analyze cost efficient and high spatial resolution images acquired by using unmanned aerial systems to understand the process of renaturation of Aa river near Münster, Germany. Among the generated products were: DSM, Orthorectified multiespectral image, orthophoto, NDVI, cloud points, 3d-mesh and image classification. This set of products was shared with the ESRI Cloud and WebGIS teams.

| Jaskaran Singh Puri | Maryeme Akhatar | Franklin Madu Chinedu | Christian Mestre


Web-portal

The purpose of the Web Portal/WebGIS group was to develop this web platform in which the obtained data of the project and related information is presented to the public. Allowing the exploration, visualization and interaction with several products. There were no restrictions concerning the technologies used and the features implemented as long as the generated data was displayed and accessible in a user friendly, efficient way. The web technologies included in the portal development were mainly Javascript, HTML, CSS, Three.js and Leaflet. The data displayed on the map are hosted on the server itself and available for download.

| André Kotze | Oluwabukola Akanbi | Guillem Ulldemolins Jornet


ESRI Cloud

The ESRI Cloud Group's main goal was to create web applications to visualize 2D and 3D data using the ESRI Commercial Platform’s products, namely ArcGIS Pro, ArcMap and ArcGIS Online. Tasks were closely related to the data acquirement by other groups using a geodatabase, producing vector and raster data visualizations (both 2D and 3D), sharing the outputs in the Web Portal, giving data access to the Webteam according to the WMS protocol, and designing and customizing the 2D and 3D ArcGIS web applications.

| Jhon Aunta | Julia Kraatz | Mareyam Belcaid


Project Management

The project management group had the responsibility of coordinating, evaluating, monitoring and providing assistance to the other groups. This included the initial task of evaluating the groups' needs, time management to meet the course deadlines and the final report submission. The organization of communication platforms like Notion, WhatsApp and Google Drive helped ensure smooth communication among the different groups.

| Daniel Erickson | Lucas Casuccio
